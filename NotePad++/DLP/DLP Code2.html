  • To establish a Data Loss Prevention (DLP) minimal viable product for Exchange and Teams chat/messaging applications using Microsoft Purview.

Purpose

  • Stronger oversight over sensitive

    information (CUI and PII data)

  • Reduces the risk of this information being exfiltrated

Enables

  • 2022 OIG Finding - OIG.2022-IT-

    C-014.2 - Ensure that the CFPB’s new DLP tool is implemented and configured to monitor traffic across all network access points and environments, as applicable.

  • NIST SP 800-171 Protecting

    Controlled Unclassified Information in Nonfederal Systems and

    Organizations

  • 5 CFR § 297.105 Agency and Office responsibilities for systems of records and applicability of the regulations

  • Exec. Order No. 13556, “Controlled Unclassified Information” (Nov. 4, 2010)

  • Controlled Unclassified Information, 32 CFR Part 2002

Justification


Test functionality of CUI and PII DLP policies/rules for

Exchange and Teams chat/messaging

Configure DLP rules to auto block sensitive information from leaving the Bureau allowing a business justification to override.


Enable user awareness of why a policy trigger occurred


Achieve oversight of sensitive information (CUI and PII) being

transmitted via Exchange and Teams)


Reduce the risk of data exfiltration in Exchange and Teams

Scope





Identify Future DLP Enhancements

  • Identify future DLP enhancements. X Demonstrate successful pilot testing

The purpose, goals and objectives of the DLP MVP for Microsoft Office Exchange and Teams has proven successful in the Test Tenant.

  • DLP policies specific to CUI & PII have been configured and internally tested for Exchange and Teams in Test

  • Users are pointed to information pertaining to why they are receiving a DLP policy violation

  • In Exchange, users must provide a business justification for transmitting sensitive information outside of the network

  • In Teams chat/messaging, users are prevented from transmitting sensitive information including attachments outside the CFPB network

  • Established through API connection DLP log ingestion into Splunk

  • Communications and Informational resources / training materials have been developed

  • Identified Future DLP iterations
