CFPB Service Desk Guide:

Register a YubiKey to Microsoft 365 Account

Security Information Access of User Account

This document covers accessing a user's account to register their YubiKey during the onboarding process. Local users can pick their equipment and remote users will have their laptop shipped.

  1.   On your laptop, log in to your ADM profile to reset the user’s password.

  2.   Log in to the user’s computer with their credentials.

  3.   Open a browser window and navigate to myprofile.microsoft.com.

    Note: Depending on the browser, Google Chrome (preferred) or Microsoft Edge, screenshots moving
    forward may vary.

  4.   Log into Office365 in your browser.

    Note: If you are already logged into Office 365 in your browser, you will see the Overview tab and you
    can skip to Step 8.

  5.   Enter the user's CFPB email address and click 'Next'.

  6.   In the Enter password window, enter the Temporary Access Pass created in Step 1.

    Note: You can check the box "Show Temporary Access Pass" to confirm the password.

  7.   Click Sign in.

             

    Note: If prompted, check the box for ‘Don’t show this again’ and click Yes to stay signed in.

  8.   Once logged in, select 'Security info' from menu on the left.

  9.   Select 'Add sign-in method'.

  10.   Choose 'Security key' from the dropdown.

  11.   Click 'Add'.

    Note: If prompted with an additional window labeled Security Key with the message 'To set up
    a security key, you need to sign in with two-factor authentication    '. Click 'Next' and
    log in with your PIV card.

  12.   Choose 'USB device'.

  13.   In the Security Key window, insert your security key, then select 'Next' The Security Key window will
    redirect you to the PIN setup wizard.

    Note: If the Create a passkey on a phone or tablet window appears, click on ‘Use a different
    device    ’ and then click on ‘External security key or built-in sensor’.

  14.   On the Security key setup prompt, click ‘Ok’ to continue.

    Note: Depending on the browser, the message stating where the request (Windows or Google)
    comes from and publisher will be stated.

  15.   In the Pin required screen, enter 17001700 (default pin) in the PIN and Confirm PIN fields and
    click Next.

    Note: If prompted with a message 'To set up a security key, you need to sign in with two-factor authentication', click Next and log in with your PIV card.

  16.   Create a new pin for the YubiKey.

    Note: It is possible to use the same PIN code as your current PIV PIN.

  17.   Click 'OK'.

  18.   Touch the metal tab (a gold strip on the side or gold circle) on your YubiKey to verify user presence
    when prompted.

  19.   Depending on your browser, click 'Allow' at the 'Allow this site to see your security key?' prompt,
    or 'OK' at the 'Continue setup' prompt to proceed.

  20.   At the Security Key prompt, create a descriptive name for your YubiKey and click ‘Next’.

    Note: There are no naming requirements.

  21.   Click ‘Done’ to complete enrollment.

  22.   Verify that the newly added YubiKey appears in the Security info section.

    Note: Provide the default PIN to the user when assisting them with setting up their new laptop during
    training or onboarding appointment. To assist a user with changing their PIN in their Windows or
    Mac device, use the CFPB Service Desk Guide: Change YubiKey PIN for End Users instructions.
