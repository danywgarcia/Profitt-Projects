CFPB End User Guide:

Windows Hello for Business Enrollment

Overview

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) has a requirement to provide phishing resistant Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all users and computers. As a result, CFPB is using a multi-facet approach to MFA options available on their Windows 11 machines. Windows Hello for Business (WHfB) replaces passwords with strong two-factor authentication on devices. The purpose of this document is to provide a step-by-step guide to assist users with enrolling into WHfB.

Enrollment Steps

  1. Log in to your CFPB Windows 11 machine with your current preferred method.

  2. The Windows Hello for Business Enrollment process will start.

    Note: If the enrollment does not start, you may need to reboot your computer and ensure
    your computer is connected the the VPN before login.

  3. Click 'Yes, setup' to continue the wizard.

    Note: If you would like to change the biometric option from facial recognition to fingerprint
    instead, click the link 'Use your fingerprint instead'.

  4. Start the biometric enrollment process using your fingerprint or camera for facial recognition.

  5. Create PIN by clicking 'Next'.

    Note: If you use a password to log in to your laptop, you will be prompted by Okta Verify to
    to send a push notification.

  6. Type PIN number and then type PIN again to confirm, click 'OK' when done.

    Note: DO NOT click the check box for 'Include letters and symbols'.

  7. Click 'OK' to finish the enrollment process.

    You're all set! No further action needed.

Enroll Additional WHfB Method (Facial Recognition or Fingerprint)

This section is designed to show you how to enroll a second factor like facial recognition in addition to your fingerprint or vice versa.

  1. After signing into the laptop, click the 'Start menu'.

  2. Use the search bar to type 'System' then Click 'Open' on the System Control panel.

  3. Click Accounts.

  4. Click 'Sign-in options'.

  5. Select the additional factor you wish to enroll.

  6. Click 'Set up' and follow the wizard for enrollment.

  7. Click 'Get started'.

  8. When prompted, enter your PIN created for Windows Hello.

  9. Follow the on screen instructions to finish enrollment.
