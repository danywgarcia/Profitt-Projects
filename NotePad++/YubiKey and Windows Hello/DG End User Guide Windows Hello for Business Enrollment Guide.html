CFPB End User Guide:

Windows Hello for Business Enrollment

Overview

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) has a requirement to provide phishing resistant Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all users and computers. As a result, CFPB is using a multi-facet approach to MFA options available on their Windows 11 machines. Windows Hello for Business (WHfB) replaces passwords with strong two-factor authentication on devices. The purpose of this document is to provide a step-by-step guide to assist users with enrolling into WHfB.

Fingerprint Recognition Method

  1. Log in to your CFPB Windows 11 machine with your current preferred method.

  2. The Windows Hello for Business Enrollment wizard will start.

    Note: If the enrollment does not start, you may need to reboot your computer and ensure
    your computer is connected the the VPN before login.

  3. Click the 'Use your fingerprint instead' link.


    Note: Facial recognition is currently limited to only the Latitude 7330 model.

  4. Start the biometric enrollment process using your finger by touching the fingerprint sensor.

    Note: This process will require you to repeatedly lift and place your finger on the fingerprint scanner.
    Follow the prompts as they will direct you on how to adjust your finger.

  5. Click Next once the process has completed and you receive the message 'That's it, you're all set!'.

  6. At the Create a PIN prompt, you will create a pin in case the fingerprint scanner authentication method
    fails. Click 'Next'.

    Note: If you use a password to log in to your laptop, you will be prompted by Okta Verify to
    send a push notification.

  7. In the 'Set up a PIN' window prompt, type your PIN number and then type the PIN again to
    confirm, click 'OK' when done.

    Note: It is possible to use the same 'PIN' code as your current PIV PIN. DO NOT check the box
    'Include letters and symbols'.

  8. Once you receive the 'All set!' message, click 'OK' to finish the enrollment process.

    You're all set! No further action needed.

Facial Recognition Method

WHfB has additional methods for signing in. The Dell Latitude 7330 is the only machine currently recommended by CFPB for FaceID.

  1. Log in to your CFPB Windows 11 machine with your current preferred method.

  2. Open the Search app (magnifying glass icon) to type 'System' then click 'Open' on the System Control
    panel.

  3. Click 'Accounts'.

  4. Click 'Sign-in options'.

  5. Select 'Facial recognition (Windows Hello)'.


  6. Click 'Set up' and follow the wizard for enrollment.

  7. In the 'Welcome to Windows Hello window', click 'Get started'.

  8. When prompted, enter your PIN created for Windows Hello.

  9. Follow the on screen instructions to finish enrollment.
