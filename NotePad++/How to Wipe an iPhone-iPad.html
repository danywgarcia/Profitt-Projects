Introduction

The following instructions lead the user through the process of wiping an iPhone or iPad clear of data and personal information in two different scenarios, password protection and password protection through iTunes.

Wiping an iPhone/iPad

When a user returns their iPhone/iPad, the device needs to be inspected physically for damage. If no damage is visible, follow the process below to wipe the device before it is returned to stock.

Note: If the device has any visual scratches or is damaged anywhere, follow the warranty/non-warranty process.

Scenario 1 – iPhone/iPad is password protected and did not wipe

This is the first approach to making sure the iPhone/iPad is cleared before placing the resource back in inventory.

  1. Log into Endpoint using your privileged account.

    Note: Please see KB0000109 for standard operating procedures, Rules of Behavior, and the PUA request form.

     

  2. Click on Devices.
  3. Click on iOS/iPad.

     

  4. Identify the International Mobile Equipment Identity (IMEI) number by dialing *#06# on the device to
    be wiped.

    Note: The device’s IMEI number(s) will display on your screen. Alternatively, the Serial Number can be used.

  5. In the Search by… field, type the IMEI number from the device to be
    wiped.

  6. Confirm and select the device.
  7. Click on Wipe.

    Note: This sends a wipe command to the phone. A message informing you that the wipe command was sent.
    Monitor the phone to ensure the wipe is completed.

Scenario 2 – Password Protected through iTunes

In this example, the iPhone/iPad device is password protected and cannot be wiped through iTunes.

  1. Press and hold the side and volume down buttons at the same time (through the flash of the Apple logo) until
    you see the Recovery Mode screen.
  2. Find out which macOS your Mac is using:
    1. On a Mac with macOS Catalina or later, open Finder.
    2. On a Mac with macOS Mojave or earlier, or on a PC, open iTunes (no apply account required).
  3. Connect your device to your computer.
  4. If a message asks for your device's passcode or to Trust This Computer, follow the onscreen steps. Get help if
    you've forgotten your passcode.
  5. Select your device when it appears on your computer.
  6. Click Restore [device].
  7. If you’re signed in to Find My, you need to sign out before you can click Restore.
  8. Get help if your iPhone, iPad or iPad Touch is unresponsive, won't turn on, or if your device doesn’t appear on
    your computer.

Additional considerations:

  • If the device needs to be swapped, provide the user the entire content of the device.
  • If the ticket is an innocent (break-fix), user only receives the iPhone device.
  • If the ticket is a request (off-board), device must be wiped, returned to stock.

Note: If you need a Macintosh laptop to perform these instructions, please see the Service Desk Manager.
